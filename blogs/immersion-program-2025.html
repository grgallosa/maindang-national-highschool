MNHS Senior High Students Complete Work Immersion Program

November 12–21, 2025 MNHS Admin

Senior High School students of Maindang National High School successfully completed their Work Immersion Program held from November 12 to 21, 2025. The week-long initiative provided learners with hands-on experience aligned with their chosen tracks and strands.

The program aimed to bridge classroom learning with real-world application, helping students develop work-ready skills, explore career pathways, and engage with local industries and professional environments.

Hands-On Learning Across Strands

  • Food and Beverage Services (FBS-NCII): Guided by Ma’am Evamae Gregore, students trained at MVW Hotel & Restaurant, gaining hospitality and customer service skills.
  • Bread and Pastry Production (BPP-NCII): Under Ma’am Jeramea Panes, learners trained at May Den Bakeshop and Unarce Bakeshop, mastering bakery operations.
  • Information and Communication Technology (ICT Strand): Supervised by Sir Jayram B. Javier, students developed Computer System Servicing skills at Oxord Computer Solution & Repair Shop.
  • Industrial Arts (SMAW-NCII): Led by Sir Rex Saculo, students trained in Shielded Metal Arc Welding with support from GSR Engine Rebuilding & Metal Craft and J & E Farm Implements.

The entire immersion program was overseen by Mr. Valentin Francisco, Head Teacher II, ensuring smooth coordination and effective implementation.

Celebrating Growth and Partnership

The program concluded with a closing ceremony on November 21, 2025, celebrating the students’ achievements and the successful collaboration between the school and its industry partners.

Through initiatives like the Work Immersion Program, Maindang NHS continues to empower learners by integrating academic instruction with real-life experience, preparing them for future careers or higher education.
